Mission Vision

A Simple Idea, Big Impact

Upcycling campus waste into learning tools.

Educycle is an Albukhary International University (AIU) 3Zero Club initiative. When our university provides free milk at the cafeteria, we secure the plastic caps, insert magnets under them, and label them with math questions to support children's math education.

🥛

Cafeteria Waste Sourcing

We collect milk bottle caps directly from the campus cafeteria, redirecting plastic away from Alor Setar landfills.

🧲

Magnetic Interactive Play

Every bottle cap features a secure magnet embedded inside and a numbered or mathematical sticker attached on top.

🧠

Tactile Mathematics

Children arrange numbers and arithmetic signs (+, -, ×, ÷, =) directly on the board, enhancing memory and math skills.

Aligned with the Global Three Zeros Movement
"Under the AIU YSBC 3Zero Club framework, our project falls under the Zero Net Carbon Emission pillar by keeping plastic waste out of landfills and upcycling them into educational materials."

The Upcycling Loop

How waste becomes education

Follow the journey of a cafeteria milk bottle cap from disposal to the hands of a young mathematician.

1

Bottle Cap Recovery

Free milk is served at the cafeteria daily. We salvage these clean high-density plastic caps directly from recovery bins.

2

Bottle Cap Washing

Every recovered bottle cap is sanitized and washed to ensure a hygienic educational counter for children.

3

Magnet Embedding

We insert small, powerful neodymium magnets inside the inner rim of each cap and seal them safely with eco-resin.

4

Math Labeling

Digit (0-9) and math symbol stickers (+, -, ×, ÷, =) are applied firmly to the top faces of the bottle caps.

5

Interactive Math

Children place the magnetic caps on the metal-backed board to visualize and solve math problems from our PDF guides.
